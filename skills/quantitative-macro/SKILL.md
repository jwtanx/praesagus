# Quantitative Macro Skill

## Objective
Create a data-driven macro research framework that uses quantitative leading
indicators, trend models, and regime classification to support asset allocation
and tactical positioning.

## Outputs
- Macro regime classification
- Leading indicator signal summary
- Growth and inflation outlook
- Asset allocation implications
- Timing and risk monitoring plan

## Required Sections
1. Executive summary
2. Indicator universe and methodology
3. Growth outlook and confidence
4. Inflation and monetary policy outlook
5. Asset implication map
6. Regime-dependent positioning
7. Monitoring triggers and model validation

## Research Rules
- Use explicit macro indicators: PMI, labor data, credit spreads, yield curve,
  money supply, consumer sentiment, and leading economic indicators.
- Specify the model or rule used for regime classification.
- Quantify the signal strength and historical hit rate when possible.
- Separate data-driven signals from qualitative policy judgment.
- Provide clear tactical guidance for equities, credit, rates, and FX.

## Output Template
- Macro regime: expansion / slowdown / stagflation / recession.
- Signal confidence: high / medium / low.
- Suggested positioning: risk-on / risk-off / rotation.
- Monitoring plan: indicator thresholds and reassessment timeline.
