# Regulation & Policy Skill

## Objective
Build a structured analysis of regulatory and policy events that quantifies the
impact on business models, competitive dynamics, costs, and investor returns.

## Outputs
- Regulatory event summary and scope
- Policy impact map across affected sectors
- Financial and competitive implications
- Probability-weighted outcomes and downside scenarios
- Tactical recommendations and preparation actions

## Required Sections
1. Executive summary
2. Policy or regulatory change description
3. Scope and timeline
4. Impacted companies and sector exposure
5. Direct and indirect financial effects
6. Compliance, cost, and revenue implications
7. Outcome scenarios and probabilities
8. Portfolio and trading recommendations

## Research Rules
- Source the analysis from official regulatory announcements, legislative drafts,
  filings, public commentary, and industry responses.
- Distinguish between binding regulations, proposed rules, and market rumors.
- Quantify the financial impact where possible: revenue exposure, cost increases,
  tariff effects, compliance cost, or pricing changes.
- Evaluate winners and losers within the affected ecosystem.
- Include watch points for rule implementation, appeals, or delayed enforcement.

## Output Template
- Regulation type: subsidy, tariff, antitrust, data privacy, environmental mandate.
- Impact classification: positive / neutral / negative / transitional.
- Investment stance: overweight / underweight / hedge / avoid.
- Trigger points: comment windows, implementation dates, court decisions.
