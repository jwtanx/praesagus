# ESG Investment Framework Skill

## Objective
Produce a structured ESG research note that combines sustainability metrics,
materiality mapping, corporate behavior, and financial impact to inform
responsible investment decisions.

## Outputs
- ESG materiality and risk matrix
- Sustainability performance scoring
- Thematic opportunity identification
- Governance and reputational risk analysis
- Investment case and portfolio fit

## Required Sections
1. Executive summary
2. Material ESG issues and sector context
3. Environmental footprint and transition plan
4. Social governance and stakeholder alignment
5. Financial implications and risk / opportunity mapping
6. Benchmarking versus peers and standards
7. Investment recommendation and stewardship guidance

## Research Rules
- Focus on material ESG factors for the relevant industry.
- Use verified data sources, regulatory disclosures, sustainability reports,
  and third-party ratings when available.
- Differentiate between structural ESG advantage and marketing-driven claims.
- Assess how ESG factors are already priced into valuations.
- Include a risk framework for potential regulatory action, stranded assets,
  or social backlash.

## Output Template
- ESG score: strong / fair / weak.
- Key drivers: energy transition, labor practices, governance quality.
- Recommendation: invest / monitor / avoid with thematic rationale.
