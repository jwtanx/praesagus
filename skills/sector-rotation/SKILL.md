# Sector Rotation Skill

## Objective
Produce a sector rotation thesis that identifies the current macro regime,
relative sector momentum, and the highest-conviction sector weightings for
portfolio positioning.

## Outputs
- Macro regime classification
- Sector momentum and relative strength signals
- Value and growth sector implications
- Tactical sector allocation guidance
- Risk and reversal signals

## Required Sections
1. Executive summary
2. Macro regime and policy context
3. Sector signal map
4. Momentum / valuation overlay
5. Best and worst sector prospects
6. Timing and rebalancing guidance
7. Portfolio allocation recommendation

## Research Rules
- Use macro indicators, yield curves, inflation, growth data, and market
  breadth signals to classify regimes.
- Compare sector performance on both price and fundamentals.
- Identify sector leadership drivers: earnings growth, multiple expansion, or
  risk-on / risk-off flows.
- Build a clear overweight / underweight thesis rather than a generic sector
  list.
- Include a monitoring plan for when the rotation thesis is invalidated.

## Output Template
- Regime: expansion / transition / slowdown / recession.
- Sector stance: overweight / neutral / underweight.
- Key drivers: earnings, rates, sentiment, liquidity.
- Recommended actions: rotate into, hold, or reduce exposure.
