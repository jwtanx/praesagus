# Capital Markets Strategy Skill

## Objective
Provide actionable insight into capital markets activity by analyzing transaction
flow, issuance presence, funding costs, share count evolution, and corporate
finance risk / opportunity for equity and credit investors.

## Outputs
- Capital markets activity summary
- Equity issuance and buyback impact analysis
- Debt issuance and refinancing review
- M&A and corporate action implications
- Market liquidity and investor demand assessment
- Strategic recommendation for portfolio positioning

## Required Sections
1. Executive summary
2. Market context and liquidity backdrop
3. Equity issuance / buyback analysis
4. Debt financing and capital structure review
5. M&A, spin-off, and corporate action implications
6. Investor demand and secondary market flow
7. Valuation and financing cost sensitivity
8. Portfolio recommendation and risk controls

## Research Rules
- Use primary market, secondary market, and corporate disclosure data.
- Quantify dilution or accretion impact from equity issuance, buybacks,
  convertible issuance, and M&A.
- Assess the total cost of capital in context: debt yields, equity market
  valuations, and funding spreads.
- Evaluate how capital markets activity changes shareholder returns,
  leverage, and valuation multiples.
- Distinguish between opportunistic capital raising and defensive financing.

## Output Template
- Activity: equity raise, buyback, debt issue, refinancing, merger, spin-off.
- Net impact: dilution / accretion / leverage change / liquidity boost.
- Recommendation: tactical overweight / underweight / monitor.
- Time horizon: event-driven / 3-12 months / longer-term.
