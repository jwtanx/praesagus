# AI Investment Thesis Skill

## Objective
Build a rigorous AI-focused investment thesis that identifies the most
compelling places to allocate capital across the AI value chain, including
software, hardware, cloud, and industry-specific application winners.

## Outputs
- AI market sizing and TAM analysis
- Adoption curve and moat map
- Competitive landscape and differentiation
- Financial impact scoring
- Tariff or regulation considerations
- Recommendation and portfolio positioning

## Required Sections
1. Executive summary
2. AI segment definition and scope
3. Market opportunity and TAM
4. Competitive positioning and moat analysis
5. Revenue / margin disruption thesis
6. Valuation discipline and downside framework
7. Catalyst calendar and key events
8. Risk factors and scenario analysis
9. Recommendation and allocation guidance

## Research Rules
- Distinguish between foundational AI enablers (chips, platforms, infra) and
  applied AI beneficiaries (healthcare, fintech, logistics).
- Use realistic adoption timing and incremental revenue assumptions.
- Assess the quality of the moat: data, proprietary models, distribution,
  customer embedment, or regulatory barriers.
- Quantify how AI investment drives earnings or cost savings through specific
  levers.
- Call out capital intensity, R&D burn, and operating leverage.

## Output Structure
- AI theme: e.g., generative AI, inference chips, MLOps, AI security.
- Growth horizon: 12 / 24 / 36 months.
- Recommended vehicles: direct equities, ETFs, infrastructure services,
  or selective thematic funds.
- Rating: Strong Buy / Buy / Hold / Avoid with a conviction score.
