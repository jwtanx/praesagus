# Regulatory Event-Driven Skill

## Objective
Provide an event-driven regulatory research framework that identifies the
financial impact of rule changes, antitrust actions, fiscal policy shifts, and
industry-specific regulation on publicly traded sectors and issuers.

## Outputs
- Event timeline and legal/regulatory catalyst map
- Affected companies and sector impact analysis
- Quantified earnings, valuation, and market access implications
- Likely outcomes and probability-weighted scenarios
- Investment and risk mitigation recommendations

## Required Sections
1. Executive summary
2. Catalyst description and timeline
3. Regulatory or legal mechanism
4. Impacted stakeholders and competitive shifts
5. Financial and valuation implications
6. Scenario probabilities and outcome mapping
7. Recommendation and trade idea

## Research Rules
- Use direct regulatory texts, filings, public statements, court documents,
  and analyst commentary.
- Quantify impact where possible: market share shifts, revenue exposure,
  compliance costs, or fines.
- Distinguish between conditional outcomes and base-case expectations.
- Use a probability-weighted framework for multi-outcome regulatory events.
- Highlight short-term volatility vs long-term structural effects.

## Output Template
- Event: rule change, investigation, merger approval, subsidy policy.
- Risk / opportunity: positive / negative / neutral.
- Recommendation: event-driven buy / hold / avoid or hedge.
- Timing: expected duration and key milestones.
