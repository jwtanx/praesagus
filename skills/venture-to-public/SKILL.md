# Venture-to-Public Skill

## Objective
Build a structured research note on late-stage private companies and IPO
candidates, evaluating growth runway, unit economics, capital needs, and
post-listing valuation dynamics.

## Outputs
- Pre-IPO growth and market fit thesis
- Unit economics and monetization path analysis
- IPO readiness and disclosure review
- Post-IPO valuation and return scenario
- Risk factors and market timing commentary

## Required Sections
1. Executive summary
2. Business model and growth narrative
3. Monetization and unit economics
4. Capital structure and funding history
5. IPO timing and market appetite
6. Post-IPO valuation comparison
7. Key execution risks
8. Recommendation and investor profile

## Research Rules
- Use investor decks, regulatory filings, press releases, and industry data.
- Quantify TAM, growth rates, margin expansion path, and cash burn runway.
- Analyze fundraising terms and dilution risk.
- Separate pre-IPO optionality from post-IPO execution realities.
- Identify valuation comps in the public market and any potential IPO "pop"
  dynamics.

## Output Template
- Company stage: late-stage private / SPAC / direct listing.
- Primary thesis: growth, defensibility, or carve-out value.
- Risk: execution, timing, valuation.
- Recommendation: participate / wait / avoid.
